Radar: ISRG vs SYK vs TER

Three-Judge Verdict

Laffont (Compounder): Which company satisfies 20%+ CAGR, expanding margins, and 70%+ recurring revenue? Only ISRG. SYK grows at 10.8% with 35% recurring. TER is nearly flat. No other company in the comp set meets all three criteria simultaneously.

Loeb (Catalyst): Which has the most discrete near-term catalysts? ISRG: three simultaneous re-rating events in 2026 (cardiac clearance ramp, Europe direct margin capture, usage-based penetration acceleration). No competitor has this catalyst density.

Hao (Technology Moat): Which has an irreplicable data moat? ISRG: 15M+ procedures of proprietary training data, instrument lock-in, and surgeon workflow dependency. Competitors would need a decade and billions of dollars to replicate. The moat is widening, not narrowing.

Bear Case Rebuttal

FIVE RISKS. FIVE SPECIFIC DATA-DRIVEN REBUTTALS.

1. Valuation (57.5x Trailing PE)

MANAGEABLE
Forward PE is 37.5x on accelerating EPS. 75% recurring revenue compounders at 17 – 20% CAGR with expanding FCF command premium multiples. SaaS peers at similar growth profiles trade at 35 – 50x forward earnings. Cash & STI of $5.93B provides additional margin of safety.

2. Competition (Hugo, Versius, Ottava)

MARKET EXPANDER
Hugo has fewer than 200 placements globally. Versius has approximately 150 systems. Ottava is delayed to 2027+. All are pre-scale with limited procedure libraries. ISRG's 15M+ cumulative procedures of training data is irreplicable. Surgeon retraining requires 100+ supervised cases, and no hospital absorbs that cost. Competitors expand surgeon interest while ISRG captures the procedures.

3. China Tariff Exposure

LIMITED
China represents less than 5% of ISRG total revenue. US manufacturing base insulates from tariff risk. The Europe direct acquisition improves geographic diversification and control simultaneously, reducing single-market concentration risk.

4. Hospital Capex Budget Pressure

SELF-SOLVING
Usage-based leasing converts the $1.5 – 2.5M capex objection into a per-procedure opex line. 28% of Q4 2025 placements (150 of 532) already on usage-based model. The mechanism that creates budget pressure simultaneously creates ISRG's solution.

5. Ion Cannibalization Risk

ADDITIVE
Ion targets a separate $3B bronchoscopy TAM with ~51% FY2025 procedure growth (144,100 procedures, up from 95,500 in FY2024). Uses proprietary single-use catheters following the identical toll-road model. Adds a second independent annuity stream. No da Vinci procedure category overlap.
