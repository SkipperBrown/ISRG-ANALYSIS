Bear Case Rebuttal

1. Valuation

MANAGEABLE

57.5x trailing. But forward PE = 37.5x on consensus EPS ~$12.06 (fetched live). 37.5x on accelerating EPS growth with 75% recurring revenue and expanding FCF is a compounder premium, not excess. GM compression is the Xi pattern — quarter 3–6 of a 6–8 quarter cycle. Recovery to 67.5–68.5% by FY2027 begins immediately. 37.5x on EPS growing toward ~$15+ by FY2028 is not expensive.

2. Competition

MARKET EXPANDER

CMR raised $1.43B to generate $48.7M revenue (PitchBook). Momentis last round: ~$5.9M convertible note. The kinematic dataset — 17M sequences, force feedback vectors, tissue compression events — is not replicable from a journal article. Surgeon retraining requirement: 100+ cases. Competitors are not disrupting the installed base. They are occasionally expanding the market at the low end. Competition is a market expansion story, not a share loss story.

3. China Tariff

LIMITED

China revenue: <5% of ISRG total. US manufacturing base. Europe direct acquisition (March 2026) simultaneously improves geographic diversification. The tariff risk is quantifiably small and partially offset by the Europe margin improvement. At worst, <1% EPS impact. Not a thesis-altering risk at current revenue mix.

4. Hospital Capex

SELF-SOLVING

28% of Q4 2025 placements are already usage-based leases (150 of 532). The capex objection is ISRG's own solution. Usage-based leasing eliminates the $1.5–2.5M upfront barrier. The bear case objection — hospitals cannot afford da Vinci — is precisely the scenario where the leasing model converts what would be a lost sale into a per-procedure toll. The bear case objection finances the bull case expansion.

5. Ion Cannibalization

ADDITIVE

Ion operates in a $3B bronchoscopy TAM. It addresses lung nodule biopsy — an indication with zero da Vinci procedure category overlap. Ion's ~51% FY2025 procedure growth is incremental to the total I&A toll revenue. The single-use catheter model replicates the same toll structure as da Vinci instruments. Ion is a second toll road, not a cannibalization risk.

6. Gross Margin Compression

SELF-RESOLVING (Xi Precedent)

-146bp in FY2025 driven by DV5 ramp costs — identical to da Vinci Xi launch pattern (FY2014–FY2016). Xi margin recovered fully within 6–8 quarters as manufacturing yields improved. DV5 launched commercially in FY2024. FY2025 is quarter 3–6 of that cycle. FY2026: 67.0–67.5%. FY2027: 68.0–68.5%. A compounder with 66% gross margins growing toward 68.5% in 24 months is not a margin compression story — it is a margin expansion story with a known start date. See Slide 14.
